Sunday's best and worst performances

Best

AL

Brady Anderson, Baltimore 

                       ab  r  h rbi bb so lob   avg
B Anderson cf           3  1  3  1   1  0   0  .297

Anderson's season ended with a record for leadoff home runs in a season (12, eclipsing Bobby Bonds' 23-year-old mark). The blast was his 50th of the season, breaking the Orioles' team record, making him the second player in baseball, after Oakland's Mark McGwire, to reach the total this season, and also breaking various laws of physics.

Pat Hentgen, Toronto 

                             ip       h   r  er  bb  so  hr    era
Hentgen (W, 20-10)            7 1/3   7   1   1   1   4   1   3.22

Hentgen had been repositioned in the Blue Jays' rotation for the chance to win 20 games, and he finished one of the best pitching seasons in Blue Jays history in a 4-1 victory over Baltimore. Hentgen became the second Toronto pitcher to win 20 (following Jack Morris in 1992) and put forth his last good argument for the Cy Young Award.
